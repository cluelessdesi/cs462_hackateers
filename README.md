# Formal Elements

Players:
  Multiplayer game, atleast 2 players are required but any more than 2 also work
  All players have the same role: steal a PC part until all items have been collected
  The Player interactions are cooperative play

Objective:
  Work as a team to steal 3 parts from the PC

Serious Objective:
  Learn about computer architecture

Proceedures:
  Starting Action:
    All players spawn in front of a gaming PC case

  Progression of Action:
    Throughout the game, each player (thief) can move freely and try to steal a part of the PC.
    Whenever they attempt to steal a part, they have to clear the enemies concealing information about the PC part.
    After that, the PC part becomes unlocked for them to take and they can acquire the part.

  Special Action:
    The players can / should interact with each other to come up with the right answer

  Resolving Action:
    Whenever the enemies near a PC part have been cleared the players have acquired one item. 
    When all enemies near all PC parts are correctly cleared and all items have been collected, the players are presented with one final question
    If they answer the final question correctly, they win

Rules:
  Restrictive Action:
    Can't access the PC parts before clearing the enemies nearby.
    Can't win the game unless all three parts have acquired.
    Questions are multiple choice, must select one right answer.

Resources:
  Hints. Each time an enemy is cleared, you get a hint. The hints only appear for few seconds.

Non-vanilla procedure / rule:
  The hints scattered across the stage should be collected as a team.

Conflict:
  Choices. The team must choose the correct answer to the question.

Boundries:
  The act of theft is only for the game, and is not encouraged in real life.

Outcome:
  The team wins/loses

Question:
  What does RAM stand for?

Answer:
  Random Access Memory
